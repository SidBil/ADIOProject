# Adio Gamification — Feature 3: Milestone Rewards

**Parent doc:** `00_north_star.md` (inherits all principles; completion-gating is mandatory)
**Time horizon:** The long arc — cumulative totals
**Last updated:** June 27, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

Milestones cover the long arc of engagement — the reward that keeps pulling when per-session novelty fades. They mark genuine cumulative progress with a special reward and a visibly filling goal. They also back up the streak: when a streak slips, an upcoming milestone still gives a reason to return.

## 2. Trigger

- **Cumulative sessions completed** — independent of streak and of accuracy.
- **Never expires; only moves forward.** This is the most forgiving possible structure and the natural complement to the streak: the streak can wind down, but milestones never can. A child who takes breaks still reaches every milestone.

## 3. Reward

- A special **milestone creature** — distinct from, and rarer than, the per-session surprise drops.
- Milestone creatures live on the **same wall** as the collection (see `01_collection.md`), so milestones feel like a "special collection within the collection," not a separate system.

## 4. Feedback: the progress bar

- A **progress bar** visibly fills toward the next milestone, gaining a chunk each completed session.
- The bar must **visibly move each session** to motivate — this constraint directly caps how wide milestone gaps can get (see spacing). A bar that looks stuck is demotivating.

## 5. Spacing

- **First milestone at session 5** — early enough that a new child gets the "I reached it!" feeling reasonably fast.
- Gaps then **widen gently but are capped around 8–10**, so each session always fills a visible portion of the bar.
- Example sequence: **5, 11, 18, 26, 34, 42…**
- Each milestone is meaningfully rarer than the last, but the bar never looks stuck.

### Why the cap matters
A gap of 5 fills the bar ~20% per session; a gap of 10, ~10% — both clearly visible. A gap of 25 fills only ~4% per session, which reads as "stuck" and kills the motivation the bar is meant to provide. For this population a bar that always visibly moves is worth more than a grand, distant goal — so we cap gaps rather than escalating to 50/100/365 as adult apps do.

## 6. Endpoint

- Milestones run for a **finite set** (~8–10 milestone creatures), then **stop**.
- After the final milestone, the **collection** (surprise creature every session) and **streak** carry ongoing engagement.
- This avoids two traps: an infinitely receding goal, and milestones quietly becoming meaningless. Milestones are a **completable** special set.

## 7. Relationship to other mechanics

- Feeds special creatures into the collection's wall — shared surface, one world.
- Provides the long-horizon pull the collection (per session) and streak (per week) don't.
- Acts as a backstop for the streak: a broken streak still leaves a visible milestone bar to fill.

## 8. Open questions / tunables

- **Exact total milestone count** (~8–10).
- **Final spacing curve** within the cap.
- Whether milestone creatures share the wall's rarity visual language or have their own distinct treatment.
- Whether the final milestone has a special "you completed the set" payoff moment.
