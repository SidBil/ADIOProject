# Adio Gamification — Feature 1: The Collection

**Parent doc:** `00_north_star.md` (inherits all principles; completion-gating is mandatory)
**Time horizon:** This session — the per-session reward
**Last updated:** June 27, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

The collection is the immediate, every-session reward. Completing a V&V session earns the child **one creature**, revealed as a surprise. Over time the child builds a visible collection that creates a "fill the set" pull back to the app.

## 2. The reward each session

- One creature per completed session.
- Earned on **completion**, never on accuracy (per north star §2).
- Revealed as a **surprise** — variable reward.
- The child always gets *a* creature. The surprise is *which one*, never *whether* — there are no "you got nothing" outcomes.

## 3. The wall

A persistent between-session screen the child can visit anytime.

- Creatures are arranged in **titled rows, one row per set** (e.g. Forest Friends, Sea Friends, Sky Friends).
- **Every slot is visible from day one.** Unlocked creatures show in full; not-yet-earned creatures appear as **silhouettes**.
- So the child always sees the whole shape of the collection: how many sets exist, how many creatures per set, and exactly what's still missing.
- **The silhouettes are the pull** — a half-filled row of shadows is a standing invitation to return.

## 4. The reveal

- The reveal animation is **calm and identical every time** — a predictable wrapper around surprising contents. This keeps the variable reward from reading as anxiety for sensory-sensitive children.
- Reveal intensity (animation, sound) should be parent/SLP-configurable (see open questions / north star §8).

## 5. Rarity

- Creatures come in **tiers** (e.g. common / rare / special).
- Rarer creatures are **visually distinct on the wall** — glow, frame, sparkle.
- Rare silhouettes may **shimmer subtly** so children can anticipate specific creatures before earning them — a wish-list effect that strengthens the pull toward a particular slot.

## 6. Duplicates

Duplicates can occur (a consequence of surprise drops + rarity).

- A duplicate **levels up / decorates a creature the child already owns**.
- This is purely **additive** — a repeat is never a wasted session.
- It requires **no decision** from the child (important for the population).
- It **rewards attachment** — the creature you've grown becomes "yours."

## 7. Set completion

- Completing a full set triggers a **calm payoff** — e.g. the set comes alive together in a little themed scene (the completed Forest set shows all its creatures in a forest).
- This gives the child a reason to *want* to finish sets, beyond filling individual slots.

## 8. Relationship to other mechanics

- **Milestones** (`03_milestones.md`) drop special, rarer creatures onto **this same wall**, so milestones feel like part of the collection rather than a separate system.
- The collection is the only mechanic that rewards *every* session regardless of timing, so it's the backstop when the streak slips or a milestone is far off.

## 9. Open questions / tunables

- **Duplicate level cap:** how many levels a creature can gain from duplicates before visible change stops.
- **Leveling style:** purely cosmetic (flourish/glow) vs. also a small animation/personality bump on the wall.
- **Rarity odds:** distribution across common / rare / special.
- **Set sizes:** how many creatures per set, and how many sets at launch.
- **Reveal config scope:** which aspects of the reveal are parent/SLP-adjustable.
