# Adio Creature Collection — Illustration Style Guide

**Tool:** Nano Banana Pro
**Last updated:** June 27, 2026

---

## Base Style Prompt

Use this as the foundation for every creature. Append the creature-specific description after it.

```
clipart style, flat vector illustration, uniform stroke weight throughout — every outline the same thickness with no tapering no variation no thick-to-thin transitions, bold solid black outlines only, zero shading, zero gradients, zero drop shadows, pure flat color fills only, vibrant saturated colors, the body of the creature uses no more than 3 flat colors — keep the body simple and let any special feature be the colorful focal point, no random accent colors on hooves legs ears or extremities unless explicitly described, transparent background with true alpha channel, simple clean shapes, centered composition, the creature fills most of the frame, no background elements, no texture, no noise, sticker ready, full body visible from head to toe including all four legs or limbs, dynamic pose with energy and movement — not a stiff frontal portrait, cute chibi proportions with a large round head relative to the body, big expressive round eyes with highlights, rosy cheeks, slight smile or playful open mouth expression, the creature has clear personality and charm
```

---

## What to always include

- `clipart style` — sets the overall register
- `flat vector illustration` — no painterly or photographic qualities
- `bold solid black outlines` — thick, consistent weight around all shapes
- `zero shading, zero gradients` — flat fills only, no cel-shading, no highlights
- `transparent background` — no background color or scene behind the creature
- `centered composition` — creature sits in the middle of the frame
- `sticker ready` — implies clean cutout silhouette potential

---

## What to always avoid

- Watercolor, painterly, textured, noisy, sketchy
- Soft or thin outlines
- Drop shadows or inner shadows
- Gradients of any kind
- Pastel or desaturated colors
- Background scenes, props, or environment elements
- More than 5 flat fill colors per creature

---

## Color direction

Colors should be **vibrant and saturated** — they will live on a warm cream (`#FFF6EF`) background inside the Adio app and should pop against it. Do not match or coordinate with the app's navy/pink/yellow palette. Think:

- Saturated grass green
- Electric teal
- Hot coral / tomato red
- Sunny lemon yellow
- Juicy grape purple
- Sky blue

Each creature gets its own 3–5 color combination. No two creatures in the same set should share the same dominant color.

---

## Rarity visual rules

| Tier | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| **Common** | Standard flat clipart. Clean, simple, no extras. |
| **Rare** | Same style, but one accent color should feel slightly more unexpected — a pop of neon, an unusual color combo. Still flat, still clipart. |
| **Special** | The whimsical feature (mushroom antlers, glowing belly, cloud body) gets the most saturated, eye-catching color in the set. The special element should read immediately as the "interesting thing." Still flat, still no shading. |

**4. Owl (Rare)**
